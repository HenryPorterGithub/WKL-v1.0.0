Ditches


Little gullies, broad sumps
Hollows where debris collects
Gutters sluicing waste
Trickles gathering combining
Gaining momentum
Tumbling along.
Gravity is a rather pure
force. A constant we live
With, take for granted
And then we fall.


Can you imagine eternity
Clinging to the thing that killed you?


Ditches are remarkable
I have leapt over broad ones
On the back of a snorting
Hunter. Brought up breathless
By a sudden view of Gatwick,
A new backwards view, the
Ass end of a transportation
Nightmare blighting the
Landscape. There was no
forewarning from that broad reach.
As unexpected as the safe flight
That view was shocking and
Thrilling as I found my geographic place.


In my neighbourhood the ditches are ,
planted with hedges and filled in so
That flooding occurs with each repetitive rain.
Dependable as clockwork. They say that
Ditches are only good for falling into.
How very english, I think.


Drainage is not uppermost in the English
Mentality. The first time I took the Chunnel
Train I marvelled at the sudden appearance
Of deep channels, drains, the moment
Daylight broke through on the french side.
Miles and miles of them. Such a wondrous
sight, such ingenuity, such forethought.
My first year in England was wet, sodden,
flooded beyond recognition. Swans sailed
Past the tip tops of park benches. Many
Roads impassable for months. Towns
unapproachable for want of a ditch or two.
Very crazy for a land of rain whipped
in from the sea, a land where forecasting
the weather remains a hilarious exercise
In fortune telling. I come from the new world
Where my inland home made a forecast as
dependable as the man who flipped his chalk
on black and white television for families
With boomer children.


Culverts, sized appropriately and connected
To the correctly sized ditches allow drainage
These systems are proven


When I lived in Chennai the flooding was horrific
Even though the monsoon failed to arrive.
They have never built a ditch or a drain since the
British left, but they have filled in the
Wetlands with garbage and built a silicon
Valley next to the Bay of Bengal. With each rain
The urine of rats and cats and dogs and humans
And monkeys and cows Is mixed with the open
defecation of all these Species and stands stagnate
in the road, for want of a system of drains.


Eventually it all evaporates or finds a way to trickle
To the sea with the help of gravity. In the meantime
We wade through, slosh through the toxic brew.
