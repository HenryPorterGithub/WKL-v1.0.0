Ruined Beauty


Sabotage and insurrection
Fascinated by tragic outcomes
The pain brings a chain
Of reactions impelling
Momentum forward
Towards the next event
Cruelty and shattered
Plans, muddled outcomes.


When molten lava hits the sea
Shards of glass are formed
And these projectiles can
Be lethally tragically
Threatening. Imagine my
Life like a tropical volcanic
Island where eruptions ruin
Best made hopes and plans.
Landscapes forever transformed
Enlarged made gross by the
Instant ignition of flammable
Material. Flows that cool slowly into
Sensual curvaceous lumps of
Hard stone, coarse and rough
Not yet weathered. Imagine
Yourself standing near the sea
When a stream of fire flows
Furiously into the wet salt water.
Imagine having your body impaled
By thousands of sharp splinters
The glass gleaming as if you have
Become a figurine of a glass
Hedgehog. Blood runs from wounds to
Mix with the sea and your tears
Are salt reminding you that some
Ancient ancestor crawled out
Of that soup to start a march
Through time and space across
A land mass that was once molten too.


Time and the elements sometimes
Smooth rough surfaces, sometimes
Cause a splinter of rock to shard off
Leaving a newly exposed face with
Razor sharp edges. Eventually rocks
Are ground down to grains of sand
So tiny they create a soft accumulation
Fit for relaxation on a sandy shore
But it takes eons for those grains to
Break down. From a molten destructive
River to rounded tumbled stone time
Lapses and memories fade. Our shared
Insignificance is never lost, but we
Do add to the dust that swirls the
Planet and mixes with the living and
The inanimate. When our minds cease
To inform our animated bodies, when
Our struggles and plans and discords
And achievements and sorrows and
Petty injustices matter no more
Our ruined beauty becomes one
With the movement of liquids born
deep within the fire and heat of gaia
