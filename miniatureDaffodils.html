Miniature Daffodils


Its a grey day but
I know outside
There are leaves unfurling
On my roses and drink peonies.
There are miniature daffodils
Primrose and grape hyacinths
Blooming in my pots.


It's a grey day but
I am experiencing
Tiny waves of chills and fever
That subside slowly
From a heavy wet mass
Of pain and misery.


It's a grey day but
This is not an easy affliction
But each time I conclude
That this is the new normal
They turn and recede
Little by little the grip is
Released. And I return to
Realise that another spring approaches.


It's a grey day but
My first spring was 68 years ago
I could do little for myself
That first spring. Helpless
I came to this life and
Helpless I will leave. I have
No illusions that my time is spent


It's a grey day but
The long drawn out process
Of my passing, my demise
Is coloured by the
Misery of an
Auto immune disorder
Grips my body and my mind


It's a grey day but
I wonder at a life lived
Robustly to the shocking
End. An exception to be sure.
