For all the good it does me

I'm a smart person. I have an IQ of 138. I am well educated, I have a post graduate degree. I am a creative person, I think creatively and have an engine that drives me to make. I am a person with a charismatic personality. I use my voice to project my ideas creatively to others.

I am a severely depressed person. I started suicidal ideation at seven and made my first attempt at nine years of age. I have lived a charmed life. I can make friends easily. I have spent quality time with really accomplished and interesting people. I have travelled and lived in many diverse cultures. I have studied amazing landscapes, intricate architectural edifices, and listened to amazing musical performances in a wide variety of cultures. I have owned wonderful domestic animals and benefited from their company. I have read an enormous number of books, both fiction and non fiction. I have mastered techniques that include cooking, gardening, sewing, painting, sculpture, printmaking and photography. I have trained several species of animals. I have become accomplished in a variety of sports, equestrian, swimming, skiing, skating and tennis.

Despite these activities I am constantly depressed and suicidal. I am lonely, angry and profoundly sad. How do these two facets of my personality sit together so relentlessly. Am I just clever enough to know the dual nature of this life? Is the futility ever present regardless of the beauty and majesty that exists in life?
