Motherload


When you have a mother
Who said she never wanted children
When you have a mother
Who always put her needs first
When you have a mother
Who changed with the wind
Every thought, need, desire and belief
Coloured by the
Company she kept
When you have a mother
Who was never ever there for you
When you have a mother
Who resented any trait
That reminded her of your father
When you have a mother
Who minimised your feelings
When you have a mother
Who could not comprehend
Your personal trajectory and
In that incomprehension
Could not guide, support or cheer
Any accomplishment
When you have a mother
Who repeated that she never ever wanted children
You could end up with an adult like me.


When you have a mother
Like mine
Who raised a child
like me
You grow up fantasising
What it would be like
To be raised by wolves


You grow up wondering
What it would be like
To be raised
By any wild creature.


I became obsessed by natural history
The physical features
The natural habitats
The eating and mating habits
The fur and feathers
That clothed these wild things
The enveloping covers
Their distinguishing features.
The flora and fauna of the natural world
Fascinated my mind. I was searching
For an anthropomorphic answer to
My childhood pain. I was searching for
Precedent for my becoming. I was
Searching for a roadmap.
I believed that by understanding
The flora, fauna and terrain of the
Natural world, I could decipher
From these clues, a way to
Navigate the emotional terrain
Of my familial realm. Although I
Can spout endless facts and bits of
relevant information on the natural world
This knowledge brought me
no closer to understanding the wounds
Inflicted by my narcissistic parent nor my
Absent father.


My understanding comes from living
In the world of human nature.
It is a harsh world where the human
Intellect has conjured tricks of tragedy
And mans inhumanity. The human world
Makes a sport of inflicting pain and humiliation.
As a species we seem more than well endowed
With an ability for desecration, a
Global destruction that encompasses all.


My mother might have been a
Particularly malevolent force in my world
But her sociopathy is repeated and
Rewarded within all society.
She was not alone, not singularly an aberration.
In evolutionary terms, the need for the
Forces exerted by such personalities has
Propelled us as a species. As apex
Predators we are not warm and fuzzy.
It is a harsh world we fight in
Harsh geography, harsh weather systems,
Harsher inequalities, harsher consequences
Harsh realities colour our existence.
How we learn to traverse this plain is a
Conjunction of our experiences,
our intellect and our emotional fortitude.
We are more than the sum total of our
Life experiences. We add to this our
Ability to reflect and conjure new meanings
Which we place upon our battle scarred
Being. We assimilate and regurgitate
A more nuanced being who has
Experienced both
good and evil
Pleasure and pain
Ease and disease
Loss and gain.


If we survive the motherload
Some of us live
Too tell the tale.
