A Basement of Despair


I drove down a shot lane
En rout to my doctor feelgood
I drove down Black horse lane
Dragging my black dog depression
Now whimpering in a corner
Of my mind. He quivers there
Not yet at rest, looking for his
Chance to leap into attack mode
He makes himself known by
Embarrassing behaviours. They
Revolve around personal hygiene.
I have not showered in over
Two weeks, nor brushed my teeth
In half a year. My front teeth are
broken shards discoloured and sharp.
I carry my humiliation like a badge
Of honour. Why do I not attend
To obvious necessity. I know my
Maternal line for generations
Succumbed to this affliction when
The black dog took over. I heard
stories of my mother torturing her
Aged Mother over an unwanted bath! I
Am grateful no one is micro managing
My slouch toward sanity. In time
I will get the energetic zing that
Will enable normal habits, I pray
On my way to my church of the
Feelgood doctor.


I pray for a canopy above, studded
With crows and kites and kestrels
With skylarks singing high and that
Ballistic band of swifts who speed
Past almost within touch yet never
Perching. I pray for a canopy of song
Laced over my mind with hawks and
Buzzards and rooks and jackdaws
I pray for magpies and blackbirds
Blue Jays and thrush. I pray for a
Pair of valiant swans who would pose
And threaten any intrusion, rushing across
my mind with necks outstretched, wings
Wildly Beating to scare the living daylights
Out of each crippling darkling thought.
I take Burdenshot Lane en route to
My feelgood church.
